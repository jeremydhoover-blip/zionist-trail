The Year is 1909

You are living in the Pale of Settlement in Poland, under the oppressive rule of the Russian Empire. Anti-Jewish pogroms are becoming more frequent and violent.

Inspired by the Zionist movement and the writings of Leon Pinsker and Theodor Herzl, you have decided to join other Jewish pioneers in making Aliyah to Eretz Yisrael (the Land of Israel).

Your destination is to establish a new settlement in Ottoman Palestine.

The journey will be dangerous, expensive, and difficult. Many have tried before - some succeeded, others perished along the way.

Little Mia Hoover sits in the cramped apartment in Warsaw's Jewish Quarter, surrounded by her loving family as they prepare for the most important decision of their lives.

Her grandmother Hannah Rubinstein carefully packs precious family heirlooms, whispering old prayers. Grandfather Tzvika Rubinstein studies maps of the Holy Land with weathered fingers that have seen too much hardship.

Papa Jeremy counts their meager savings while Mama Tali mends their traveling clothes by candlelight. The neighbors Iris Goldstein and Chaim Goldstein have also decided to join them - together, they will face the unknown.

The pogroms are getting worse. The family knows they must leave everything behind for a chance at freedom in Eretz Yisrael. But who will make this dangerous journey with brave little Mia?
